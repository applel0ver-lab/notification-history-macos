## The problem

A notification pops up. You're busy. You swipe it away. It's gone forever.

macOS has no notification history. Once a banner disappears from the screen, there's no way to get it back — not in Notification Center, not anywhere.

## What this does

`notification-history` is a menu bar app that silently catches every notification as it arrives and saves it to a searchable log. Miss a message, a reminder, or a calendar alert — find it in seconds.

- Saves every notification automatically in the background
- Search by app, keyword, or date
- Click any entry to open the source app
- Never lose a notification again

## How it works

1. Install and launch — the app starts capturing immediately
2. Click the menu bar icon to open your notification log
3. Browse, search, or filter by app
4. Click any notification to jump straight to it

That's it. No setup, no configuration required.

## Menu bar log

```
🔔  Today — 14 notifications

  Messages     Sarah: "Are you coming tonight?"       2 min ago
  Calendar     Team standup starts in 15 minutes      18 min ago
  Mail         Re: Invoice #1042                      1 hr ago
  Slack        #general — new message from Alex       2 hrs ago
  Reminders    Buy groceries                          3 hrs ago

  [Search notifications...]        [Clear history]
```

## Features

**Search** — find any notification by keyword, app name, or date range.

**Filter by app** — see only Messages, only Calendar, only Mail — whatever you need.

**Click to open** — every notification links back to the original app and content.

**Auto-cleanup** — set how long to keep history: 7 days, 30 days, or forever.

**Privacy mode** — pause logging any time from the menu bar icon.

## Requirements

- macOS 13 Ventura or later
- Apple Silicon or Intel

## Privacy

All notifications are stored locally on your device. Nothing is sent to any server. You can clear your entire history at any time from the menu bar.
