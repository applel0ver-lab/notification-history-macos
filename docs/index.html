Installation Guide

Step-by-step installation via Terminal

  1. ⌘

    Open Terminal

    Press ⌘ + Space to open Spotlight, type Terminal and press Enter.

  2. 2

    Install notification-history

    Paste this command and press Enter. Homebrew will download and install the app — you may be prompted for your Mac password.

    Terminal — Install notification-history
    $ brew install --cask notification-history

    On first launch macOS may ask for Notifications access — this is required to capture notifications.

  3. ✓

    Done

    When the command finishes, open notification-history from Applications or Spotlight (⌘ + Space). The app starts logging immediately — click the menu bar icon to browse, search, and open any saved notification.

About this app

notification-history is a menu bar app that catches every notification as it arrives and saves it to a searchable log — so nothing disappears when you dismiss a banner.

Everything stays on your Mac. Nothing is sent to a server, and you can clear your history anytime from the menu bar.

Automatic capture

Notifications are saved in the background as they arrive.

Search & filter

Find entries by keyword, app, or date range.

Click to open

Jump from a log entry straight back to the source app.

Retention & privacy

Choose how long to keep history; pause logging whenever you want.
